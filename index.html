Selected Cases

POH Application Logo

Creation of a Visual Identity and UI elements for a fitness and wellness application. The name "POH" (Flow) alludes to movement, breath, and continuity.

The Challenge: To visualize "Flow" without using generic fitness tropes. The key constraint was creating a symbol that retains high legibility and character when scaled down as a mobile App Icon.
The Solution: A minimal "P" monogram combining geometric structure with a fluid curve. The high-contrast design and blue-violet gradient ensure the icon remains distinct and recognizable even on crowded mobile screens.

Undisplayed Brand Identity Project

Evolution of a personal brand identity. Moving from illustrative distortion to structural clarity, this project introduces a cohesive system designed for longevity and cross-platform versatility.

The Challenge: To execute a strategic rebranding that moves away from the previous retro, distorted aesthetic which felt "dated" and limited in application. The goal was to establish a timeless and versatile visual system that better reflects the professional adaptability required in modern graphic design contexts.
The Solution: I developed a minimalist geometric wordmark and a responsive "U" monogram, both constructed on a precise grid system to ensure balance and consistency. The high-contrast color palette (Vibrant Orange & Dark Navy) combined with clean typography (Asty Cf) creates a bold, professional identity that is easily adaptable across various digital and print mediums.

Commemorative Art Book

To design a commemorative art book celebrating the centennial of legendary Greek composers Mikis Theodorakis and Manos Hatzidakis.

The Challenge: To design a commemorative art book celebrating the centennial of legendary Greek composers Mikis Theodorakis and Manos Hatzidakis. The core objective was to visualize the auditory experience of "Entechno" (Art Music) for a younger audience, avoiding a traditional, "dated" historical aesthetic. The challenge lay in translating lyrical rhythm into visual form while maintaining readability within a complex layout system.
The Solution: I developed a visual system that uses abstract, flowing forms to represent musical rhythm. The layout was carefully structured to guide the reader's eye through the content, ensuring that even complex compositions remain accessible and engaging.
